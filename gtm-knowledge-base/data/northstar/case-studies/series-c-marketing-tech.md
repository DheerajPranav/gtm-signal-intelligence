# Case Study: Adloom

> *Fictional customer for training/demo purposes.*

**Company:** Adloom — a Series C marketing-technology company (cross-channel ad optimization), ~600 employees, Salesforce + BigQuery.
**Modules:** Pipeline Analytics, Forecast Accuracy.
**Headline result:** Forecast accuracy improved by **+19 points** and pipeline hygiene disputes largely eliminated.

## The problem

Adloom's forecast swung wildly quarter to quarter. Sales and finance kept **different numbers** and reconciled them in a painful weekly meeting. Much of pipeline review was spent disputing whether deals were real rather than deciding what to do about them. A new CRO wanted forecasting discipline fast.

## Why Northstar

A **new CRO** (a strong [ICP](../sales/icp-definition.md) trigger) wanted one number the whole company trusted. Because Northstar models in **BigQuery**, the forecast reconciled with what finance and the data team already saw — ending the two-numbers problem. Warehouse-native kept [security review](../product/security.md) short.

## What changed

- Made Northstar the single [source of truth](../sales/positioning.md) reconciled against BigQuery, so sales and finance stopped keeping separate numbers.
- Adopted [Pipeline Analytics](../product/module-pipeline-analytics.md) hygiene scoring to settle "is this deal real" before review, not during it.
- Ran the [Forecast Accuracy](../product/module-forecast-accuracy.md) submission workflow with forecast-vs-actual scoring.

## Results

- **Forecast accuracy: +19 points** over two quarters.
- Pipeline-review disputes largely eliminated; reviews focus on deal strategy.
- One reconciled number across sales and finance.

> "One number, reconciled with the warehouse. The reconciliation meeting just… ended." — CRO, Adloom *(illustrative)*
