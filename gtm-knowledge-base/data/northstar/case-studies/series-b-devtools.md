# Case Study: Forgestack

> *Fictional customer for training/demo purposes.*

**Company:** Forgestack — a Series B developer-tools company (CI/CD & build infrastructure), ~230 employees, HubSpot + BigQuery.
**Modules:** Pipeline Analytics, Forecast Accuracy.
**Headline result:** Stood up a trustworthy revenue reporting layer from scratch in **5 weeks**, as the company's first RevOps hire built the function.

## The problem

Forgestack had grown fast on product-led signups but was building a real sales motion for the first time. There was **no RevOps function and no source of truth** — pipeline lived in HubSpot, usage data in BigQuery, and the forecast was a founder's gut feel. As deal complexity grew, that stopped scaling.

## Why Northstar

Forgestack's first RevOps leader, **Jordan Whitfield**, was hired to build the function end to end. Rather than assemble a BI stack by hand, Jordan wanted an opinionated [RevOps analytics layer](../product/overview.md) that connected HubSpot and BigQuery out of the box and matched a Series B budget ([Core → Growth](../product/pricing.md)).

## What changed

- Connected HubSpot + BigQuery and deployed in **5 weeks**.
- Used [Pipeline Analytics](../product/module-pipeline-analytics.md) to define stages, hygiene rules, and a single pipeline source of truth.
- Introduced the [Forecast Accuracy](../product/module-forecast-accuracy.md) submission workflow so the forecast became a measured process, not a guess.

## Results

- A working **single source of truth** for pipeline and forecast in just over a month.
- Weekly forecast now has a measured accuracy history instead of gut feel.
- RevOps function scaled with the sales team without a data-engineering project.

> "Northstar was the fastest way to stand up RevOps without hiring a data team first." — Jordan Whitfield, Head of RevOps, Forgestack *(illustrative)*
