# Why Your Forecast Is Wrong (and How to Actually Measure It)

Most revenue teams can tell you this quarter's forecast. Almost none can tell you how accurate *last* quarter's forecast was, or why it missed. That's the real problem — not the number, but the absence of a feedback loop.

## Accuracy you don't measure can't improve

If you never score the forecast against what actually closed, every quarter starts from zero. You re-argue the same deals, re-learn the same lessons, and present a number to the board with no track record behind it. The fix is unglamorous: **score every forecast against actuals, every quarter**, and watch the error shrink.

That's the core idea behind Northstar's [Forecast Accuracy module](../product/module-forecast-accuracy.md). Reps commit through a submission workflow. The AI provides a transparent second opinion — not an override — and every past forecast is graded against what really happened. Accuracy becomes a measured metric, not a feeling.

## From ~70% to 90%+ in two quarters

The teams we work with typically start around **70% accuracy** and reach **90%+ within two quarters** simply by closing the loop. Ledgerly went from [68% to 91%](../case-studies/series-c-fintech.md). Adloom improved [+19 points](../case-studies/series-c-marketing-tech.md). The gain doesn't come from a smarter model alone — it comes from finally *measuring* the thing you're trying to improve.

## Transparency beats black boxes

A forecast you can't interrogate is a forecast you can't trust. When leadership asks "why is the number down?", you should be able to expand it into the deals and weights behind it in seconds — not shrug at a model you can't see into. That's why we build warehouse-native and keep every number traceable. (It's also our main contrast with [Clari](../sales/battlecard-clari.md).)

## Where to start

Pick one quarter of live pipeline and score your last three forecasts against what closed. If you can't — that's the gap. A [14-day pilot](../sales/sales-playbook.md) closes it on your own data.
