# Pipeline Hygiene Is a System, Not a Nag

Every sales org has the same ritual: a manager pings reps to "clean up the pipeline" before the review. Deals get nudged, dates get pushed, and everyone shows up Monday to argue about which opportunities are actually real. Hygiene-by-nagging doesn't scale — and it burns the first half of every review.

## The cost of a dirty pipeline

A pipeline full of stale, stalled, and wishful deals doesn't just look messy — it **poisons the forecast**. If half your review is spent deciding what's real, you're not inspecting deals, you're doing data cleanup in a meeting with your most expensive people in the room. Teams routinely lose **~6 hours a week** to review prep alone.

## Make hygiene a scored, standing signal

The alternative is to treat hygiene as a **system**: define what "healthy" means (recent activity, realistic close dates, required fields, stage-appropriate progress), then score every deal against it continuously. Now the messy deals surface *before* the review, automatically, and the meeting starts from a clean board.

That's what Northstar's [Pipeline Analytics module](../product/module-pipeline-analytics.md) does — hygiene scoring and deal inspection on top of your Salesforce/HubSpot pipeline, modeled in your warehouse so the numbers reconcile with what your data team sees. Review prep drops from **~6 hours to ~30 minutes**.

## Clean pipeline, better forecast

Hygiene and forecasting are the same problem from two angles. A clean pipeline is the raw material for an accurate forecast; that's why teams that fix hygiene usually see [accuracy climb](blog-forecast-accuracy.md) right after. Adloom [eliminated its "is this deal real" disputes](../case-studies/series-c-marketing-tech.md) and the reconciliation meeting simply ended.
