# Sales Playbook

How Northstar runs a deal, from first touch to close. Grounds in the [ICP](icp-definition.md) and [positioning](positioning.md).

## Target & trigger

Prospect against the [ICP](icp-definition.md). The strongest trigger is a **recent RevOps/Sales Ops leader hire** at a **Series B–D, 200–2000 employee B2B SaaS** company on **Salesforce/HubSpot + Snowflake/BigQuery**. Secondary triggers: new CRO, fresh funding round, public mentions of "pipeline hygiene" or "forecast accuracy."

## Prospecting message (SDR)

Lead with the pain, not the product: spreadsheet forecasting, distrusted numbers, review prep eating the week. Reference the specific trigger (e.g., "saw you just hired a Head of RevOps"). CTA: a 20-minute working session, not a demo.

## Sales stages

1. **Discovery** — run the [discovery questions](discovery-questions.md); confirm ICP fit and the primary pain (forecast vs hygiene vs productivity). Identify the economic buyer (usually VP RevOps or CRO).
2. **Technical validation** — confirm CRM + warehouse; loop in the data team; scope the warehouse-native option. Address [security](../product/security.md) early.
3. **Proof-of-value pilot** — 14 days on a slice of live pipeline with one agreed success metric (usually forecast accuracy or review-prep time). This is our differentiator: **buy on your data, not our demo.**
4. **Business case & pricing** — quantify ROI from review-prep savings and forecast credibility; present [pricing](../product/pricing.md) (Core/Growth/Enterprise).
5. **Close** — align on rollout plan; 4–6 week implementation; hand off to Customer Success.

## Multi-threading

Map the deal to all four [personas](icp-definition.md): VP RevOps (champion/economic), CRO (economic), Head of Sales Ops (champion/admin), VP Sales (user). Single-threaded deals on a VP RevOps alone are our most common slip — get the CRO in.

## Competitive plays

Expect [Clari](battlecard-clari.md) in enterprise-leaning deals, [Gong Forecast](battlecard-gong.md) where Gong is already in, [Mosaic](battlecard-mosaic.md) when finance is driving, and [Pigment](battlecard-pigment.md) in planning-led evaluations. Lead with warehouse-native transparency and time-to-value.

## What "good" looks like

- Champion can articulate the forecast-accuracy pain in their own words.
- Data team is bought in on warehouse-native.
- A pilot success metric is agreed **before** the pilot starts.
- CRO is engaged by technical-validation stage.

## Handling stalls

If a deal stalls, revisit the trigger and quantify the cost of the status quo (hours lost, board credibility). Use the [objection-handling](objection-handling.md) guide. Related: [FAQ](faq.md), [case studies](../case-studies/series-c-fintech.md).
