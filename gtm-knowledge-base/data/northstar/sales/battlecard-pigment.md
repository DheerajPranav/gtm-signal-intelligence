# Battlecard: Northstar vs Pigment

## Who they are

Pigment is a **business planning / EPM (enterprise performance management)** platform — a flexible modeling canvas for sales, finance, and workforce planning. Powerful and general-purpose.

## Where they win

- Extremely **flexible modeling** — you can build almost any planning scenario.
- Strong for enterprise **sales planning** (capacity, quota, territory) and finance planning.
- Handles complex, bespoke planning needs well.

## Where we win (lead with these)

- **Opinionated and fast-to-value.** Pigment is a blank canvas — power comes with **months of configuration and a modeling owner**. Northstar ships opinionated pipeline hygiene, forecasting, and productivity out of the box and deploys in **4–6 weeks**.
- **Operational, not just planning.** Pigment models the plan; Northstar runs the weekly operations — deal-level hygiene, forecast submission, forecast-vs-actual scoring. Different job.
- **Warehouse-native at the deal grain.** Northstar models opportunities from Salesforce/HubSpot in Snowflake/BigQuery. Pigment is a modeling layer you feed.
- **Lower lift for a RevOps team.** No need to hire or dedicate a planning-tool admin to get value.

## Traps to set

- "How long did it take to configure Pigment to something usable, and who owns the models?"
- "Does it do operational pipeline hygiene and a rep-facing forecast submission workflow, or is it planning/modeling?"
- "What happens when the model needs to change — who does that and how fast?"

## Objections you'll hear

- *"Pigment can model anything."* — True, and that flexibility is the cost: time, expertise, and an owner. For operational RevOps at Series B–D, opinionated and fast beats infinitely flexible and slow.
- *"We want one planning platform."* — Northstar isn't a planning platform; it's the operational analytics layer. They can coexist — Pigment for planning, Northstar for running the number.

## One-liner

**Pigment is a flexible planning canvas you configure for months; Northstar is an opinionated RevOps analytics layer live in weeks.**
