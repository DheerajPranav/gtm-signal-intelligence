# Battlecard: Northstar vs Mosaic

## Who they are

Mosaic is a **strategic finance / FP&A platform**. It connects financial and operational systems to help finance teams plan, budget, and report. Increasingly it reaches toward revenue metrics.

## Where they win

- Strong with **finance / FP&A** buyers; excellent for budgeting, headcount, and financial planning.
- Good at cross-functional financial roll-ups and board reporting from a finance lens.
- Established in the office of the CFO.

## Where we win (lead with these)

- **Owned by the revenue team, not finance.** Northstar is built for the VP RevOps, Head of Sales Ops, CRO, and VP Sales. Mosaic is finance-owned; RevOps often can't get the operational, deal-level views they need.
- **Operational, not just planning.** Northstar does pipeline **hygiene**, deal inspection, forecast **submission workflow**, and rep productivity — day-to-day revenue operations. Mosaic is planning and financial analysis, a level up and to the side.
- **CRM + warehouse native at the deal grain.** Northstar works at the opportunity level from Salesforce/HubSpot; Mosaic works at the financial-metric level.
- **Faster for RevOps' actual job.** A RevOps leader trying to fix forecast accuracy and pipeline hygiene gets there faster with Northstar.

## Traps to set

- "Who owns Mosaic internally — finance or RevOps? Can RevOps inspect individual deals and run pipeline hygiene in it?"
- "Is this solving weekly pipeline/forecast operations, or quarterly financial planning?"

## Objections you'll hear

- *"Finance already bought Mosaic."* — Perfect for FP&A. It doesn't do operational pipeline hygiene, deal inspection, or a rep-facing forecast submission workflow — that's RevOps' job and Northstar's home.
- *"Isn't this overlap?"* — Minimal. Mosaic plans the financial model; Northstar runs the revenue engine feeding it. Many customers have both.

## One-liner

**Mosaic is finance's planning platform; Northstar is the revenue team's operational analytics layer.**
